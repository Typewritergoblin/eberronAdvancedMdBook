# Exploring Eberron: Subclasses

Source: *Exploring Eberron* (2024), Chapter 6: Character Options

---

## Artificer: Forge Adept

*Craft a Mighty Weapon out of Your Soul*

The daashor artificers of the Dhakaani Empire were the finest weaponsmiths in Khorvaire's history. As a Forge Adept, you carry on these ancient traditions, creating mystical weapons and armor, strengthening allies on the battlefield, and bringing down enemies with weapons imbued with your own soul energy.

### Level 3: Tool Proficiency

You gain proficiency with Smith's Tools. If you already have this proficiency, you gain proficiency with one other type of Artisan's Tools of your choice.

In addition, when you craft an ordinary or magic weapon, the amount of time required to craft it is halved.

### Level 3: Forge Adept Spells

| Artificer Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Armor of Agathys, Shield of Faith |
| 5 | Spiritual Weapon, Warding Bond |
| 9 | Beacon of Hope, Remove Curse |
| 13 | Death Ward, Fire Shield |
| 17 | Banishing Smite, Wall of Force |

### Level 3: Battle Ready

**Arcane Empowerment.** When you attack with a magic weapon, you can use your Intelligence modifier, instead of Strength or Dexterity, for the attack and damage rolls.

**Weapon Knowledge.** You gain proficiency with Martial weapons. You can use a weapon with which you have proficiency as a Spellcasting Focus for your Artificer spells.

### Level 3: Ghaal'Shaarat

As a Magic action while you have Smith's Tools in hand, you can turn a Simple or Martial Melee weapon into a Ghaal'Shaarat. The weapon continues to be a Ghaal'Shaarat until you imbue a different weapon or you die.

The weapon gains the following benefits while you wield it:

**Mighty Blade.** A nonmagical weapon becomes a magic weapon. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with the weapon, unless it already had this bonus or a better one.

**Returning Weapon.** The weapon has the Thrown property with a normal range of 30 feet and a long range of 120 feet. It returns to your hand immediately after it is used to make a ranged attack roll.

### Level 5: Extra Attack

You can attack twice instead of once whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

### Level 9: Runes of War

You gain the following benefits while wielding your Ghaal'Shaarat:

**Improved Mighty Blade.** You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with the weapon, unless it already had this bonus or a better one.

**Aura of War.** As a Magic action, you begin radiating a bolstering aura in a 30-foot Emanation. Choose one damage type: Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Thunder. While in the aura, you and your allies each deal an extra 1d4 damage of the chosen type when hitting with a weapon attack or Unarmed Strike. The aura lasts for 1 minute, until you use this feature again, or until you end it (no action required). Uses equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1); all recharge on Long Rest.

### Level 15: Perfect Weapon

**Perfect Mighty Blade.** You gain a +3 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with the weapon, unless it already had this bonus or a better one. Additionally, the first time you attack with your Ghaal'Shaarat on your turn, you can transfer some or all of the weapon's bonus to your Armor Class. The bonus remains until the start of your next turn.

**Elemental Strike.** When you hit a creature with an attack roll using your Ghaal'Shaarat, choose one damage type: Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Thunder. Your Ghaal'Shaarat deals an extra 1d6 damage of the chosen type; and you can cause the weapon to deal the chosen type of damage or its normal damage type.

**Soul Bound.** You have Resistance to Psychic damage, and you have Immunity to the Charmed and Frightened conditions.

### Level 20: Weapon of Legend (Alternate Capstone)

While wielding Ghaal'Shaarat: Aura of War is always active. **Retributive Aura:** enemies starting their turns in your aura take damage equal to the aura's extra damage dice. **Empower Aura (Bonus Action):** increase aura to 2d6 damage for 1 minute; uses equal to Intelligence modifier; all recharge on Long Rest.

## Artificer: Maverick

*Craft the Tool You Need in the Moment*

A Maverick Artificer is always tinkering, forever chasing a new idea. Where other Artificers focus on a single path of arcane science, the strength of the Maverick is flexibility.

### Level 3: Tool Proficiency

You gain proficiency with Tinker's Tools. If you already have proficiency, you gain proficiency with one other type of Artisan's Tools of your choice.

In addition, when you craft a magic item, the amount of time required to craft it is halved.

### Level 3: Maverick Spells

You learn one cantrip of your choice from the Artificer, Cleric, or Wizard spell list (Intelligence-based). In addition, any time you prepare Artificer spells, you can prepare additional spells equal to your Proficiency Bonus, chosen from the Artificer, Cleric, and Wizard spell lists.

### Level 3: Arcane Prototype

You create Arcane Prototypes — experimental constructs that function like Spell Scrolls (only you can use them).

**Arcane Charges:** You have a pool of Arcane Charges. You can imbue spells into prototypes up to the Max Spell Level shown in the Arcane Prototypes table. Casting from a prototype requires normal casting time; once cast, it crumbles to dust unless you expend a spell slot equal to the spell's level to preserve it.

**Creating Prototypes:** When you finish a Long Rest with Tinker's Tools in hand, you can create prototypes by expending Arcane Charges (one per spell level).

| Artificer Level | Max Prototypes | Max Spell Level | Arcane Charges |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| 5 | 2 | 2 | 3 |
| 9 | 3 | 3 | 5 |
| 13 | 3 | 4 | 7 |
| 15 | 4 | 5 | 9 |
| 17 | 4 | 6 | 11 |
| 20 | 5 | 6 | 13 |

### Level 5: Fine Tuning

Whenever you cast an Artificer spell while you have Tinker's Tools in your possession, you can add your Intelligence modifier to one damage roll of the spell.

### Level 9: Work in Progress

Whenever you finish a Short Rest, you can replace one of your prepared spells with a new spell from the Artificer, Cleric, or Wizard spell list.

### Level 15: Final Breakthrough

Whenever you finish a Short Rest, you can swap a spell contained in your Prototype for another spell of the same level, chosen from the Artificer, Cleric, or Wizard spell list.

In addition, you ignore all class, species, spell, and level requirements on attuning to or using a magic item.

### Level 20: Improved Genius (Alternate Capstone)

Magic action: cast any spell of level 8 or lower without meeting any requirements (including costly components). Once per Long Rest.

## Bard: College of the Dirge Singer

*Bolster your Allies with Story and Song*

Students of both history and war, Dirge Singer Bards use tales of past glory to inspire their allies and provide keen strategic insight. This college was born in the Empire of Dhakaan, but over the course of the Last War, Cyre developed its own version of these techniques.

### Level 3: Broad Inspiration

You learn the Guidance cantrip (Bard spell, doesn't count against cantrips known).

Additionally, when you confer a Bardic Inspiration die to a creature, you can also confer that Bardic Inspiration die to a second creature within range.

### Level 3: Keeper of History

You gain proficiency in the History and Performance skills. If you already have these proficiencies, you instead gain Expertise.

### Level 6: Commanding Voice

When you take the Attack action or the Magic action on your turn, two creatures of your choice who possess one of your Bardic Inspiration dice can expend it to use their Reaction to immediately make one attack with a weapon or an Unarmed Strike, adding the Bardic Inspiration die to the attack's damage roll on a hit.

### Level 14: Battle Hymn

As a Bonus Action, you can start a Battle Hymn that can be heard by all creatures within 60 feet. The hymn lasts until the start of your next turn. While the hymn is active, whenever an ally within 30 feet who can hear you makes an ability check or a saving throw, they can add 1d4 to the roll.

## Cleric: Mind Domain

*Channel Divine Devotion into Psychic Power*

The Mind Domain celebrates the power of the mortal mind, channeling psychic energy to protect the faithful and smite enemies. Most often associated with the Path of Light or the Riedran Path of Inspiration.

### Level 3: Mind Domain Spells

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Command, Detect Thoughts, Dissonant Whispers, Mind Spike |
| 5 | Clairvoyance, Fear |
| 7 | Confusion, Phantasmal Killer |
| 9 | Synaptic Static, Telekinesis |

### Level 3: Psychic Feedback

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of yourself succeeds on a Wisdom saving throw, you can take a Reaction and expend a use of your Channel Divinity to impose Disadvantage on the saving throw, prying into their mind before they succeed or fail.

### Level 3: Psychic Force

When you cast a Cleric spell that deals Radiant damage, you can choose to have it inflict Psychic damage instead. When you cast a spell that deals Psychic damage, you can add your Charisma modifier to that spell's damage against one of the spell's targets.

### Level 6: Gestalt Anchor

You project a sense of calm in a 10-foot Emanation (inactive if you have the Incapacitated condition).

You and your allies in the projection gain a +2 bonus to Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws.

A creature can only benefit from one Gestalt Anchor at a time.

### Level 17: Bend Reality

When an ally you can see within 30 feet of yourself fails a saving throw, you can replace the roll with a 20, potentially changing the outcome. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.

## Druid: Circle of the Forged

*Shape-shift into Beasts of Steel and Wood*

Druids who embrace the Circle of the Forged explore the potential of the warforged form, blending animal shapes with warforged durability.

### Level 3: Skin of Steel

When you assume a Wild Shape form, you gain the following benefits:

**Challenge Rating.** The maximum CR for the form equals your Druid level divided by 3 (round down).

**Armor Class.** Your AC equals 15 plus your Wisdom modifier if that total is higher than the Beast's AC.

**Construct Resilience.** You have Resistance to Poison damage. You have Advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the Poisoned condition.

**Tireless.** You don't gain Exhaustion from dehydration, malnutrition, or suffocation. You don't need to sleep, and magic can't put you to sleep.

**Obvious.** Your body is made from the same materials as a warforged; your muscles are rootlike tendrils protected by armored plates.

### Level 3: Circle of the Forged Spells

| Druid Level | Prepared Spells |
|---|---|
| 3 | Blade Ward, Identify, Mirror Image |
| 5 | Haste |
| 7 | Fire Shield |
| 9 | Animate Objects |

### Level 6: Elementally Imbued

While in a Wild Shape form, you gain:

**Elemental Strikes.** Each of your attacks can deal its normal damage type, Acid, Cold, Fire, or Lightning damage (choose each time you hit).

**Elemental Cascade.** Choose one type: Acid, Cold, Fire, or Lightning. Once per turn when you hit with a Wild Shape attack, you can expend a spell slot (no action) to deal extra damage equal to 1d6 per spell slot level (max 5d6). The target makes a Constitution saving throw (your spell save DC):
- **Acid (fail):** -½ Wisdom modifier penalty to AC (minimum 1) for 1 minute
- **Cold (fail):** Speed 0, Disadvantage on Strength and Dexterity checks for 1 minute
- **Fire (fail):** Takes Fire damage equal to your Wisdom modifier at start of each turn for 1 minute
- **Lightning (fail):** Can't make Opportunity Attacks; first attack each turn has Disadvantage for 1 minute

The target repeats the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.

### Level 10: Adamantine Hide

While in a Wild Shape form, you gain Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage.

In addition, when you are hit by an attack roll or forced to make a saving throw, you can take a Reaction and expend a use of Wild Shape to shape-shift into one of your Wild Shape forms.

### Level 14: Construction Perfection

While in a Wild Shape form:

**Armor Class.** Your AC equals 16 plus your Wisdom modifier if that total is higher than the Beast's AC.

**Iron Mind.** You have Immunity to the Charmed, Frightened, Paralyzed, Petrified, and Poisoned conditions, and Poison damage.

## Monk: Warrior of the Living Weapon

*Reshape Your Body for Battle*

Warriors of the Living Weapon pursue physical perfection, honing their body as a smith sharpens a sword. Their traditions are drawn from cultures of shape-shifters and creatures who fight with tooth and claw.

### Level 3: Martial Form

At the start of your turn, you can expend 1 Focus Point to manifest your martial form (lasts 10 minutes, until used again, or until Incapacitated). Choose one:

**Forged Heart.** Whenever you hit an object with an Unarmed Strike, the hit is a Critical Hit. Whenever you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike granted by Flurry of Blows, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 15 feet directly away from you.

**Nightmare Shroud.** When you use Deflect Attacks, the attacking creature must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the creature has the Frightened condition until the end of its next turn.

**Traveler's Blade.** Your reach increases by 10 feet.

**Weretouched.** When you reduce an enemy within 5 feet of you to 0 Hit Points, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to one roll of your Martial Arts die plus your Monk Level.

### Level 3: Mutable Strike

Whenever you deal damage with your Unarmed Strike, it can deal your choice of Bludgeoning, Piercing, or Slashing damage.

### Level 6: Manifest Blow

You gain one of the following options (matching your Level 3 choice):

**Forged Heart.** Once per turn when you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike, you can deal extra damage equal to one roll of your Martial Arts die.

**Nightmare Shroud.** As a Magic action, you can expend 2 Focus Points to cause psychic energy to erupt from you. Each creature of your choice in a 30-foot Emanation must make a Wisdom saving throw. Failed save: Psychic damage equal to three rolls of your Martial Arts die + Frightened condition until end of your next turn. Successful save: half damage only.

**Traveler's Blade.** Once per turn when you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike, you can use both the Damage and the Grapple option. You are always considered to have a free hand for Grappling purposes.

**Weretouched.** When you hit the same creature twice on your turn with Unarmed Strike, you can expend up to 3 Focus Points to deal extra damage on your second hit equal to a number of Martial Arts die rolls equal to the Focus Points expended.

### Level 11: Reflexive Adaptation

When you use Self-Restoration, you can remove these additional conditions: Blinded, Deafened, and Grappled.

In addition, when you finish a Short Rest you can remove 1 level of Exhaustion from yourself; when you finish a Long Rest you can remove all levels of Exhaustion from yourself.

### Level 17: Perfect Form

Whenever you assume your Martial Form, you can expend an additional 3 Focus Points to gain for the duration:

**Mercurial Strikes.** Whenever you attack with an Unarmed Strike granted by Flurry of Blows, you have Advantage.

**Regeneration.** At the start of your turn, roll one Martial Arts die. You regain that many Hit Points.

**Unbreakable Body.** You gain a +2 bonus to your Armor Class.

---

> **Warrior of the Living Weapon Background Notes:**
> - **Forged Heart** — Warforged tradition; physical evolution of constructed frames
> - **Nightmare Shroud** — Kalashtar tradition; ectoplasmic projections, manifests claws of shadow, usually surrounded by image of quori spirit
> - **Traveler's Blade** — Tribal changeling tradition; grow bone blades, knuckle ridges, or stretch limbs
> - **Weretouched** — Shifter tradition; techniques developed by shifter champions of Olarune
