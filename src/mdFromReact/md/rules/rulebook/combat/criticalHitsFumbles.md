# Critical Hits and Fumbles

This system uses a cinematic Critical Hits and Fumbles system. 


## Critical Hits

On attacks that roll a 20, roll the 1d20 again to confirm the critical. (Even if you had advantage on the original attack, you only roll 1 die to confirm your critical.) Based on your roll, you may modify your critical:

| Confirm Roll | Critical |
| --- | --- |
| 1-9 | Regular Critical. Double dice involved in the critical. |
| 10-19 | Heroic Critical - Describe an event along with a minor game advantage in addition to critical damage. (Roughly every 40 attacks) |
| 20 | Legendary Critical - Describe a significant event with a major game advantage in addition to critical damage. (Roughly every 400 attacks) |

### Heroic Criticals

A Heroic critical allows a player to add a cinematic flair to a scene by either adding an additional effect to their attack, changing something in the environment that has equal impact on both sides, or allowing a teammate to act on their turn. These are designed to create "cinematic" moments that can shape the battle.

Players can choose an effect (preferred), randomly roll for it, or let the DM decide. These are simply examples of criticals - you may propose one of equal power.

| Type of Effect | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Physical impact | Knock the target hit by the attack prone, Push or pull the target 10' in any direction, knock the target off balance, grapple the target. |
| Distracting Effects |  Taunt the target (disadvantage to against everyone but attacker), try to disarm the target. |
| Teamwork effects | Give an ally advantage on saves from the target until start of next turn, give ally temporary HP equal to your HD, give ally advantage on next attack or give target penalty on next attack or saving throw, let an ally move around 15', Grant ally attack of opportunity. |
| Environmental change | Catch something on fire, collapse a roof, create difficult terrain all around you, or another change that impacts both sides equally. |
| Crunchy Crit | Deal max damage on 1 weapon die and roll the extra crit die normally. |


### Legendary Criticals

A Legendary Critical is the result of a 20/20 roll. A legendary critical allows a player to dictate a major scene changing effect. This typically is an autokill, but it can also be any other effect of similar power or importance to the scene. This is chosen by the player. 

| Type of Effect | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Lethal Strike | The target of the attack is immediately killed. If target is a PC or named NPC, target is instead reduced to 0 HP (although they may sustain an injury at DM's discretion). Will not work if target is immune to damage dealt by attack. |
| Incapacitating Blow | The target is knocked unconscious, disarmed, or stunned with no save.  |
| Mass attack | Instead of killing a single target, score a critical on multiple targets adjacent to either you or the target. |
| Environmental Effect | Catch something on fire, or collapse a roof, as above - but this can impact the other side much more than your own. This can be used to help your allies retreat, or do something else that impacts your entire side. |
