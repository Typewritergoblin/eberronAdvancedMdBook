## Eberron Cultures

There are numerous cultures that your character might hail from. Choose a nation and a faction from within that nation, and then choose skills, languages, and feats appropriate to your culture.

### Khorvaire
#### The Five Nations
The core human-dominated nations were once united under the great Kingdom of Galifar. Following the century-long Last War, each modern nation emerged with distinct cultural hallmarks and simmering rivalries.

##### Aundair
Aundair's culture is steeped in arcane scholarship, education, and magical excellence. It is the historical seat of the Arcane Congress and the floating towers of Arcanix. Rather than relying on massive armies, Aundairian culture values mystical artillery, cunning wordplay, spirited debates, and the widespread use of everyday magic in both agriculture and daily life. Its people are acutely fashion-conscious, preferring bright colors and feathered hats.

##### Breland
Breland is the industrial heart and pragmatic center of Khorvaire. Historically the seat of the King's Dark Lanterns (Galifar's intelligence agency), Brelish culture is heavily associated with covert operations, cosmopolitan commerce, and a scrappy, independent spirit. The nation's sprawling metropolis, Sharn, exemplifies its commitment to industry, vertical architecture, and personal freedom, which often breeds a cynical populace and a thriving underworld.

##### Cyre (The Mournland)
Before its destruction, Cyre was the crown jewel and central capital of Galifar, intentionally drawing on the cultural strengths of all the other nations to create something new. Its people were known for their artistic versatility, prosperity, and the "Cyran appreciation" philosophy. Today, the nation is a twisted, monster-filled wasteland, and its surviving refugees form a displaced culture clinging to their lost heritage across Khorvaire under the leadership of Prince Oargev.

##### Karrnath
Karrnath is defined by a harsh, stoic, and fiercely martial culture. It boasts the oldest military traditions on the continent, historically housing the prestigious Rekkenmark military academy. During the Last War, this grim stoicism was supplemented by desperate necromantic innovation driven by the Blood of Vol, leading to the widespread use of intelligent undead forces to bolster their ranks on the battlefield. 

##### Thrane
Thrane is a devoted theocracy guided by the Church of the Silver Flame. Its culture is highly martial, but driven by religious zeal and a desire to purge supernatural evil rather than secular discipline. Archery is considered a foundational devotional practice. This results in a society that is deeply devout, ascetic, and community-focused, though occasionally prone to extreme fanaticism.

#### Darguun
A harsh nation of goblinoids born from a mercenary betrayal during the Last War. Darguun struggles with stability, as its disparate cultures are only loosely held together by the military might and intimidation of the Lhesh Haruuc.

##### The Ghaal'dar
The Ghaal'dar are the dominant lowland tribes, characterized by a stoic warrior culture where hobgoblins maintain rigid order. They rule over lesser goblins through force and manage physically superior bugbears through tactical intellect. They form a fragile alliance of tribes, with the Rhukaan Taash ("Razor Crown") at the forefront.

##### The Marguul
The Marguul are savage, xenophobic tribes inhabiting the Seawall Mountains of southern Darguun, strictly dominated by bugbears. They keep other races, including smaller goblins and hobgoblins, as slaves. While some pay homage to the Lhesh Haruuc, others, like the Kalkor clan, worship the Mockery and violently attack any outsiders who enter their territory.

##### The Heirs of Dhakaan (Kech Dhakaan)
These are the true inheritors of the ancient goblinoid empire who isolated themselves in deep subterranean vaults to escape the daelkyr's madness thousands of years ago. They have recently returned to the surface, bringing unparalleled martial discipline, exceptional smithing that rivals House Cannith, and a strict, honor-bound society completely distinct from surface goblins. Their spiritual leaders are the female dirge singers known as the duur'kala.

#### Droaam
A monstrous frontier governed by the enigmatic Daughters of Sora Kell. Droaam serves as a haven for diverse and historically oppressed species. It is a land where minotaurs, harpies, medusas, oni, the Znir Pact gnolls, and the pacifistic Gaa'ran orcs live together. Instead of "wide magic," their society relies on "wide monster", finding civic and industrial applications for the supernatural gifts of their diverse citizenry, such as using gargoyles as couriers or medusas as architects and magistrates.

#### The Eldeen Reaches
A vast expanse of forests and plains that rebelled against Aundairian rule, defined by its deep, abiding ties to the natural world and primal magic.

##### Agricultural Farmers
The eastern plains of the Eldeen Reaches are populated by fiercely independent farmers and homesteaders. They seceded from Aundair to protect their way of life, forming a grounded, community-focused culture that relies on cooperation, local militias, and a deep respect for the land.

##### Woodland Inhabitants
The deeper, ancient forests are home to scattered shifter tribes and powerful druidic sects. Factions like the Wardens of the Wood, the militant Ashbound, the apocalyptic Children of Winter, and the fey-aligned Greensingers guide the spiritual and physical protection of the region, viewing modern civilization with varying degrees of suspicion or outright hostility.

#### The Lhazaar Principalities
A loose, treacherous confederacy situated along the northeastern coastline, defined by a culture of nautical prowess and ruthless ambition.

##### Seafaring Princes
The majority of the Principalities are composed of fiercely independent merchant sailors, privateers, and pirate lords. Their culture revolves around individual freedom, naval superiority, and the endless pursuit of wealth and glory on the high seas, where a captain's worth is measured entirely by their ship and crew.

#### The Mror Holds
The ancestral homeland of the dwarves is a feudal confederation located in the freezing Ironroot Mountains. Twelve wealthy ruling clans dominate the culture through intense economic feuding, extensive mining operations, and the financing of Khorvaire's banking systems via House Kundarak. Much of their modern culture is also driven by exploring the ruined subterranean Realm Below, sparking a severe cultural divide between traditionalists and clans like Soldorak who embrace the dark, organic symbiont weapons of the daelkyr Dyrrn the Corruptor.

#### Q'barra
A perilous, swampy frontier region divided between desperate human colonists and ancient native empires.

##### New Galifar and Hope
These regions are settled by human refugees, wandslingers, and idealists who fled the Last War, hoping to maintain the old laws of Galifar or forge new, independent lives. It is a rough frontier culture focused on survival, community building, and highly lucrative Eberron dragonshard prospecting.

##### Indigenous Cultures
The deep jungles are home to proud, ancient reptilian societies. This includes various lizardfolk tribes, such as the Cold Sun Federation and the fiend-worshiping Poison Dusk. It also houses honorable dragonborn clans who view themselves as the sacred protectors of ancient ruins and draconic secrets dating back to the Age of Demons.

#### The Shadow Marches
A desolate, bleak swampland defined by a blend of tribal traditions and the lucrative Eberron dragonshard trade.

##### Marcher Clans
The central culture of the Marches is a unique, centuries-old integration of human immigrants from Sarlona and native orc tribes. These integrated clans operate the powerful House Tharashk and have developed a distinct, unified swamp culture built on family loyalty, survival, and prospecting.

##### Insular Tribes
Deeper in the swamps are isolated tribes that refuse integration. These include the ancient Gatekeeper druids, who maintain primal traditions to keep the daelkyr sealed beneath the earth, as well as darker, twisted sects that worship the maddening Cults of the Dragon Below.

#### The Talenta Plains
Vast, open grasslands inhabited by nomadic tribes of halflings. Rejecting the industrialization of the Five Nations, they maintain ancient traditions, roaming the plains on dinosaur mounts (such as clawfoot raptors), sporting distinct tribal masks, and relying on shamans to commune with the spirits of the natural world.

#### Valenar
A militaristic nation established on former Cyran soil by militant elves from Aerenal during the chaos of the Last War.

##### The Tairnadal Elves
Organized into fierce warclans, these warrior elves rule the nation. Their culture is singularly focused on seeking glory in battle to perfectly emulate and honor their patron ancestors, viewing peace as a failure of their sacred duty. They are renowned for their exceptional horsemanship and devastating cavalry charges.

##### Human Peasantry
Beneath the ruling elves is a subjugated population of human peasant farmers, originally Cyran citizens. The elves care little for administration, leaving the humans largely to govern their own agricultural lives as long as they provide resources and food for the roaming elven warclans.

#### Zilargo
A beautiful, seemingly idyllic nation of gnomes known for their pursuit of knowledge, academia, and the mastery of elemental binding. The culture is highly diplomatic, cheerful, and social, but this harmony is secretly and strictly enforced by an omnipresent, omniscient secret police known as the Trust, who eliminate threats before they ever manifest through espionage and assassination.

#### The Demon Wastes
A barren wasteland in northwestern Khorvaire, twisted by the dark powers of ancient fiends.

##### The Carrion Tribes
Savage, nomadic tribes of humans and orcs who worship the dark spirits and entombed rakshasa rajahs of the Wastes. Their lives are filled with perpetual warfare, blood sacrifice, and cannibalism.

##### The Ghaash'kala
Fierce clans of orcs and humans who act as paladins and guardians of the Labyrinth. They are sworn to a sacred duty to prevent the evils of the Demon Wastes from escaping into the rest of Khorvaire, often fighting a tragic, endless war against the Carrion Tribes and fiends.

### Sarlona
#### Riedra
A sprawling, xenophobic empire of strict communal, agricultural, and military order. It is ruled by the Inspired, human hosts possessed by manipulative quori dream spirits from Dal Quor. Riedran culture is a dystopian caste system where the populace is kept docile, entirely isolated from the outside world, and controlled via massive psychic monoliths known as hanbalani.

#### Adar
A harsh, mountainous refuge in the south of Sarlona. It is inhabited by human monks and the kalashtar (humans bonded to rebel dream spirits fleeing Dal Quor). They practice the Path of Light, a philosophy of martial and psychic discipline, maintaining a powerful psychic shroud to defend their fortress monasteries from constant Riedran sieges.

#### Syrkarn
A free, agrarian land of wind-carved steppes. It is populated by human clans descended from Riedran refugees and eneko (mongrel half-giants descended from the ancient kingdom of Borunan). It is a harsh but open culture of survival, horsemanship, and nomadic movement, where humans and eneko live together in remarkable harmony.

#### The Tashana Tundra
The frozen northern reaches of Sarlona, plagued by strange reality storms and bitter cold.

##### Nomadic Shifters
The tundra is home to diverse, hardy shifter nations like the Qiku, Saartuk, and Chuniigi, who survive the elements through strong community bonds, shamanistic magic, and primal traditions. 

##### Dwarves and Maenads
The region also houses the militant refugee Akiak dwarves fighting a guerrilla war of vengeance against Riedra, the isolationist Dorann dwarves, and the mysterious, psionic Inana maenads, all of whom have adapted unique survival cultures in the ice.

### Aerenal
#### The Aereni
The dominant culture of this heavily forested island continent. These elves possess incredibly advanced magical abilities and a deep, encompassing reverence for the dead. Rather than mourning, they preserve their greatest citizens as deathless undead or spirit idols to serve on the Undying Court, which guides their highly traditional, isolationist society. They value the perfection of ancient techniques over modern innovation.

#### The Tairnadal
Residing primarily in the northern steppes of Aerenal (and overlapping with the nation of Valenar), the Tairnadal are martial elves divided into the Valaes Tairn, Silaes Tairn, and Draleus Tairn. They believe they must literally anchor their ancestors to the world by emulating their glorious, bloody deeds in combat.

### Xen'drik
#### The Giants
Tens of thousands of years ago, giants ruled an incredibly advanced magical empire. Now reduced to savagery by ancient draconic curses that warp the land, tribes of hill, stone, frost, and fire giants live as barbarians, scavenging among the colossal, cyclopean ruins of their former civilization.

#### The Drow
Created by the ancient giants from enslaved elves to serve as assassins, the dark elves of Xen'drik are completely unlike those in other realms, divided into three distinct cultural sects.

##### The Vulkoori
Tribal, jungle-dwelling hunters who primarily worship a scorpion deity known as Vulkoor. They utilize chitin armor and scorpion venom, practicing primal magic and holding an eternal vendetta against the giants who once enslaved them.

##### The Sulatar
Also known as the Firebinders, the Sulatar remained loyal to the promises of their giant masters. They reside in hidden obsidian cities, utilizing highly advanced elemental binding techniques and fire magic, waiting for a promised land of fire.

##### The Umbragen
Dwelling deep in the subterranean caverns of Khyber beneath Xen'drik, the Umbragen are bound to a shadowy, magical force known as the Umbra. They use incredibly advanced, dark magic to fight an endless, losing war against aberrations and daelkyr horrors.

#### Stormreach
An eclectic and lawless port city built atop ancient giant ruins on the northern peninsula. Originally established as a pirate hideout, it now serves as a rough-and-tumble cosmopolitan gateway for Khorvairian explorers, scholars, and dragonmarked houses looking to plunder the shattered continent.

### Argonnessen
#### The Dragons
The undisputed masters of the continent, dragons maintain a mysterious, ancient, and immensely powerful civilization. Their society is divided into distinct, advanced territories such as the tradition-bound Thousand Flights, the martial Light of Siberys, the philosophical Tapestry, and the chaotic proving grounds of the Vast, all dedicated to interpreting and manipulating the Draconic Prophecy.

#### The Seren Barbarians
Human and dragonborn barbarian tribes that inhabit the islands and northern coast of Argonnessen. They act as fierce, zealous guardians of the continent, worshiping the dragons as divine totems or ancestors and slaughtering any outsiders who attempt to reach the mainland.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Frostfell & Everice
#### The Ice Folk and Barbarians
Primitive human neanderthals and everfrost barbarians who eke out a nomadic or semi-nomadic existence along the frigid northern and southern ice caps. Their culture is strictly focused on survival, subsisting heavily on fishing and seal hunting in the deadly cold.

#### Glacier Dwarves (Tol dun Nordorthak)
Known as "Those Who Stayed," ancient dwarven clans inhabit frozen keeps and glacial strongholds in the Frostfell. Believed by some historians to be the original ancestral homeland of all Khorvairian dwarves who later migrated beneath the earth, they maintain a highly isolated, hardy civilization centered around the freezing extremes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Khyber (The Underdark)
#### The Daelkyr and Aberrations
Khyber is less a unified culture and more a prison for the alien lords of madness from Xoriat. The daelkyr and their twisted creations, such as mind flayers, chokers, and dolgrims, carve out bizarre, maddening subterranean kingdoms, endlessly plotting to spread their chaotic, warping influence to the surface.

#### Subterranean Civilizations
Deep below the earth exist ancient, isolated societies completely cut off from the sun. This includes the sealed vaults of the Heirs of Dhakaan, tribes of blind grimlocks and troglodytes, irvhir kobolds, and the shadow-bound Umbragen drow fighting for their survival.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### The Thunder Sea
#### The Eternal Dominion
A massive, advanced, and highly militaristic civilization of sahuagin that dominates the ocean floor. They worship the Devourer and believe in their divine right to conquer through cunning and courage, heavily utilizing underwater alchemy, mutation, and transmutation magic. Their sprawling cities are built around massive, slumbering creatures known as kar'lassa (great dreamers).

#### Karakala (The Kalamer)
Nomadic tribes of merfolk who were originally drawn to Eberron from the planar waters of Lamannia during planar convergences. Free-spirited and deeply magical, they utilize ancient druidic rituals to stabilize erratic manifest zones in the upper waters of the Thunder Sea, serving as neutral intermediaries between the sahuagin and the sea elves.

#### The Valraean Protectorate
A buffer state established by the Undying Court off the coast of Aerenal, populated primarily by sea elves (the line of Valraea), locathah, and assimilated sahuagin. While they present themselves as glorious, semi-divine protectors, the reality is they are an oppressive regime that actively stamps out native locathah and sahuagin cultures. They strictly enforce elven supremacy, Aereni society, and worship of the Undying Court to hold the line against the expansion of the Eternal Dominion.
