
## Languages

### I. Common Languages of Khorvaire
These languages are widely spoken across the continent of Khorvaire, facilitating trade and diplomacy.

| Language | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Common** | The primary trade language of the Five Nations, introduced by human settlers from Sarlona. |
| **Common Sign Language** | A sign language used by many Common speakers; widely recognized in the Five Nations. |
| **Goblin (Ghukliak)** | The dominant language of the ancient Dhakaani Empire; spoken in Darguun, Droaam, and the Shadow Marches. |
| **Dwarvish** | Spoken in the Mror Holds; Mror dwarves also speak Common for trade. |
| **Elvish** | Spoken by the elves of Aerenal and Valenar; often learned by scholars and arcanists. |
| **Gnomish** | The native tongue of Zilargo. |
| **Halfling** | Spoken by the nomadic tribes of the Talenta Plains. |

### II. Exotic and Regional Languages
These languages are tied to specific cultures, distant continents, or isolated communities.

| Language | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Draconic** | The language of dragons; often used in arcane magic and academic studies. |
| **Riedran** | The common tongue of Sarlona, derived from Old Common but altered by Inspired rule. |
| **Quori** | The language of the Inspired, kalashtar, and the spirits of Dal Quor. |
| **Draconic** | The language of dragons and kobolds; the language of arcane magic used by wizards and scholars alike. |
| **Giant** | The language of the ancient giant civilizations of Xen’drik; widely spoken by drow and explorers in that region. |
| **Argon** | A pidgin dialect of Common spoken by the barbarians of Seren Island and Argonnessen. |
| **Gnoll** | Spoken by the Znir Pact gnolls; relies on pitch and ultrasonic vocalizations. |
| **Orc** | Largely a dead language in civilized Khorvaire, replaced by Goblin; still spoken by isolated tribes like the Jhorash’tar of the Ironroot Mountains and the clans of the Shadow Marches. |
| **Sahuagin** | The language of the sea devils of Shargon’s Teeth and the Eternal Dominion. |
| **Aquan** | Spoken by aquatic races like the Kalamer (merfolk) and water elementals. |
| **Undercommon** | Spoken by denizens of Khyber and the Realm Below. |


### III. Planar and Ancient Languages
These languages originate from the planes or the dawn of time.

| Language | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Abyssal** | The language of demons and the Lords of Dust; often called "Khyber's Speech". |
| **Celestial** | The language of angels and couatls; often called the "Tongue of Siberys". |
| **Daelkyr (Deep Speech)** | The language of the Lords of Madness (Xoriat) and aberrations. |
| **Primordial** | The language of elementals; dialects include Auran, Aquan, Ignan, and Terran. |
| **Sylvan** | The language of the fey of Thelanis and the Greensinger druids. |
| **Infernal** | A common language among fiends; also spoken by tieflings of the Venomous Demesne. |


### IV. Unique and Secret Languages
These languages are specific to certain organizations or subcultures, often requiring specialized training or physiology to use.

| Language | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Druidic** | The secret language of druids, originally taught by the dragon Vvaraak. |
| **Thieves’ Cant** | A coded language used by the criminal underworld of Khorvaire. |
| **Skin Cant** | A visual language used by changelings to convey messages through shifting skin patterns. |
| **Stonespeech** | An artificial, condensed language created by House Sivis for use with speaking stones. |
| **High Kaluu** | A blend of Draconic, Infernal, and Riedran spoken by the tieflings of the Venomous Demesne. |
| **Stonegrind** | A language used by gargoyles involving the grinding of stone surfaces. |
| **Tauric** | The language of the minotaur clans of the Barrens (Droaam). |
| **Wuura’gaa** | The language of the worgs of the Watching Wood. |
| **Serpentine** | A language used by the medusas of Cazhaak Draal involving hissing and snake-hair movement. |

### V. Obscure Planar Dialects
Ancient texts and scholars may refer to specific languages tied to the planes, though in modern times these are often subsumed by Primordial, Celestial, or Abyssal.

| Language | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Daan** | Spoken by formians and modrons of Daanvi. |
| **Irial** | Spoken by ravids and lights of Irian. |
| **Kythric** | Spoken by slaadi of Kythri. |
| **Mabran** | Spoken by nightshades and shadows of Mabar. |
| **Risian** | Spoken by ice-based creatures of Risia. |
| **Syranian** | A dialect of Celestial spoken by angels of Syrania. |
