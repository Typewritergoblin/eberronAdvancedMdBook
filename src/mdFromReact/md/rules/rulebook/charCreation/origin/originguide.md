# Character Origins

<!-- toc -->

Every adventurer in Khorvaire has a past — a web of experiences that shaped who they are before they ever drew a blade or spoke an arcane word. In this campaign, a character's origin is built from three interlocking components: **Species**, **Background**, and **Culture**. Together these elements establish who your character is, where they came from, and what they knew before the adventure began.

**Origin Steps:**
1. [Choose a Species](#step-1-choose-a-species)
2. [Choose a Background](#step-2-choose-a-background)
3. [Choose a Culture](#step-3-choose-a-culture)
4. [Choose Languages](#step-4-choose-languages)

---

## Origin Components

Each part of your origin reflects a different layer of your character's identity. Species determines what you are. Background reflects what you did. Culture captures where you were shaped — the nation, faction, or community that left its mark on your worldview, skills, and loyalties.

---

## Step 1: Choose a Species

Your species determines your character's fundamental physical and magical nature. This includes your creature type, size, speed, and the special traits your biology or magical heritage grants you.

Khorvaire is home to humans, elves, dwarves, halflings, gnomes, half-elves, half-orcs, and the many peoples native to Eberron — including the warforged, changelings, shifters, and kalashtar. Each brings distinct traits to the table.

### Parts of a Species

**Creature Type.** All playable species in this campaign are Humanoid unless otherwise noted.

**Size.** Your species determines whether you are Small or Medium. Some species allow you to choose.

**Speed.** Your species determines your base movement Speed.

**Special Traits.** Each species grants a set of special traits that reflect its unique physiology or magical nature. You gain all traits listed for your species.

> See the [Species](../../../../../../mdFromReact/md/rules/rulebook/charCreation/origin/species.md) section for all available options.

## Step 2: Choose a Background

Your background represents the occupation, environment, or station that defined most of your life before you became an adventurer. A soldier who fought in the Last War, a House agent navigating dragonmarked politics, an inquisitive working Sharn's lower wards — each background tells a story.

### Parts of a Background

**Ability Scores.** Each background lists three ability scores relevant to that background. Increase one by 2 and another by 1, or increase all three by 1. No score may exceed 20 from this increase.

> This campaign uses the **Community Array** (17 : 15 : 13 : 13 : 11 : 9) as the base. Ability scores are uncapped and may exceed 20 through other advancement. See [Ability Scores](./abilityScores.md) for full details.

**Feat.** Your background grants one Origin feat, representing a foundational talent or training from your earlier life. Origin feats are described in the [Feats](../feats.md) chapter.

**Skill Proficiencies.** Each background grants proficiency in two skills drawn from its narrative. Skill proficiency adds your Proficiency Bonus to relevant ability checks. See [Skills and Specializations](../skillsAndSpecializations.md) for full skill definitions, including the Eberron-specific skills Academics, Channel, Commune, and Culture.

**Tool Proficiency.** Each background grants proficiency with one tool — either a specific tool or one chosen from a relevant category. Tools are described in [Tool Proficiencies](./toolsProficiency.md).

**Equipment.** Each background offers a choice between a starting equipment package suited to that background or 50 GP to spend as you see fit.

> See the [Backgrounds](./backgrounds.md) section for all available options. Backgrounds from the Player's Handbook, Forge of the Artificer, Frontiers of Eberron, and Faiths & Frontiers are all available.

---

## Step 3: Choose a Culture

Culture is an additional origin identity that reflects the deep impact the region and culture you grew up in had on your development. Where your background tells us what you did, your culture tells us where and among whom you were shaped.

A child raised in the arcane scholastic society of Aundair grew up differently than one forged in the brutal tribal discipline of Darguun — even if both became soldiers. Culture captures that difference.

> See [Eberron Cultures](./culture.md) for descriptions of Eberron's nations and cultural factions to help guide your choices.

### Culture Skills and Feats

Once you have chosen a culture, pick the following to represent your connection with them:

**2 Additional Skills or Tools.** Choose two skills or tool proficiencies that reflect the knowledge and practices common to your culture. Use your judgment to select options that fit — a Brelish criminal underworld upbringing might suggest Culture and Thieves' Tools, while a Mror Holds dwarven clan childhood might point toward History and Mason's Tools. These can include the new Academics, Commune, Channel, Culture skills from the skill list.

**1 Additional Feat.** Choose one General feat that reflects a talent, tradition, or discipline common to your culture. This represents the bonus General Feat granted at 1st level for this campaign. Choose something that fits your character's cultural background rather than purely optimizing for combat.


---

## Step 4: Choose Languages

Languages are accumulated across all three origin components. By the end of character creation, your character will know a number of languages drawn from their species, background, and culture combined.

**From Your Species.** Your species grants you Common plus one additional language tied to your people — Elvish for elves, Dwarvish for dwarves, and so on. Some species may grant additional languages or offer a choice.

**From Your Background.** Some backgrounds grant one or more additional languages as part of their narrative. Check your chosen background for details.

**From Your Culture.** Your culture reflects the region and community that shaped you. Work with your DM to choose languages that fit your cultural origin — a character raised in Aundair might know Elvish from its scholastic traditions, while one from Darguun might know Goblin as a second tongue alongside Common. Your culture does not grant a fixed language, but the DM may suggest one or more appropriate to your region.

> See [Languages](./languages.md) for the full list of available languages, organized by type.

## Putting It Together

| Step | Choose | What You Gain |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | Species | Creature type, size, speed, special traits, Common + 1 species language |
| 2 | Background | Ability score increases, Origin feat, 2 skill proficiencies, 1 tool proficiency, starting equipment or 50 GP, possible additional languages |
| 3 | Culture | 2 additional skill or tool proficiencies, 1 General feat |
| 4 | Languages | Any additional languages from species, background, and culture combined |

Once you have completed your origin, move on to selecting your [Class](../classes/class.md) to determine your adventuring abilities.
