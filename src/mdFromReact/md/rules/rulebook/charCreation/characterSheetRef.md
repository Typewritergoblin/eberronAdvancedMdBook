## Before You Begin

Talk with your DM about the tone and setting of the campaign. This game is set in **Eberron** — a world of arcane industry, dragonmarked politics, and the long shadow of the Last War. Think about where your character fits in that world before you commit to your choices.

---

## Step 1: Choose a Class

Your class defines what your character *does* — their training, talents, and role in the party. See the [Class Guide](./classes/class.md) for descriptions of all available classes and subclasses, including a summary table.

**Record on your sheet:** Class name, Hit Die, armor training, and proficiencies from your class.

> **Level 0:** Before your first class level, your starting HP equals your **Constitution score**. Once you take Level 1, HP advances normally using your class Hit Die. To play a Level 0 character, skip class selection and go to Step 2 to select your Origin and Culture.

---

## Step 2: Determine Your Origin

Your origin comprises three parts: **Species**, **Background**, and **Culture**. Together they establish who you are, where you came from, and what shaped you. See the [Origin Guide](./origin/originguide.md) for the full breakdown.

### Species
Choose a species from the [Species list](./origin/species.md). Record your size, Speed, and all special traits.

### Background
Choose a background from the [Backgrounds list](./origin/backgrounds.md). Each background provides:
- Ability score increases (one by 2 and one by 1, or all three by 1 — none above 20)
- One Origin [Feat](./feats/featsOrigin.md) or Dragonmark [Feat](./feats/featsDragonmark.md)
- Two skill proficiencies
- One tool proficiency
- Starting equipment or 50 GP

### Culture
Choose a culture from [Eberron Cultures](./origin/culture.md) that reflects where your character grew up. Culture provides:
- Two additional skill or tool proficiencies
- One additional General feat
- Possible additional languages (discuss with your DM)

See [Languages](./origin/languages.md) for the full language list. Your character knows Common plus languages from their species, background, and culture.

---

## Step 3: Determine Ability Scores

This campaign uses the **Community Array**: **17 : 15 : 13 : 13 : 11 : 9**

Assign these six scores to Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. Prioritize your class's primary ability. Then apply your background's ability score increases.

Ability scores are **uncapped** in this campaign — they can exceed 20 through advancement.

**Ability Score Modifiers:**

| Score | Modifier |
|:---:|:---:|
| 8–9 | −1 |
| 10–11 | +0 |
| 12–13 | +1 |
| 14–15 | +2 |
| 16–17 | +3 |
| 18–19 | +4 |
| 20 | +5 |

See [Ability Scores](./origin/abilityScores.md) for full details.

---

## Step 4: Record Proficiencies and Skills

Your **Proficiency Bonus** at level 1 is **+2**. Apply it to:
- Saving throws listed by your class
- Skills granted by your class, background, and culture
- Tools granted by your background and culture
- Weapons and armor your class provides training in

See [Skills and Specializations](./skillsAndSpecializations.md) for the full skill list, including the Eberron-specific skills: **Academics**, **Channel**, **Commune**, and **Culture**.

If you have both proficiency in a skill *and* a relevant tool, you have **Advantage** on checks using both.

---

## Step 5: Choose Starting Equipment

Your class and background each provide a starting equipment package, or you may take the GP equivalent and buy your own from the equipment lists. Record everything on your sheet.

---

## Step 6: Fill In Remaining Details

With your choices made, complete your character sheet:

- **Hit Points:** Roll your class Hit Die (or take the average) and add your Constitution modifier. Remember Level 0 HP = Constitution score.
- **Armor Class:** Determined by your armor and Dexterity modifier.
- **Initiative:** Equal to your Dexterity modifier.
- **Saving Throws:** Note which are proficient from your class.
- **Spells (if applicable):** Record your spell save DC and spell attack bonus from your class description.
- **Feats:** Record your Origin feat (from background), your General feat (from culture), and any additional feats from your class or species.

---

## Step 7: Describe Your Character

Give your character a name, appearance, and personality. Consider:

- What nation or faction did you grow up in?
- What did you do before you became an adventurer?
- What drove you to take up this life?
- How do your species, background, and culture intersect in how you see the world?
