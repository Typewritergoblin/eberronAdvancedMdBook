### Bard: College of Whispers

#### Psychic Blades
*3rd-level College of Whispers feature*

You can make your weapon attacks magically toxic to a creature's mind. When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one use of your Bardic Inspiration to deal an additional 2d6 psychic damage to that target. You can do so only once per round on your turn.

The psychic damage increases when you reach certain levels in this class, increasing to 3d6 at 5th level, 5d6 at 10th level, and 8d6 at 15th level.

When it takes this psychic damage, the target's mind is filled with doubt and fear. Choose one of the d6's you rolled for the damage. The creature must subtract the value of the chosen die from the next attack roll, saving throw, or ability check it makes in the next 10 minutes. If the creature is immune to psychic damage, this feature has no effect.

#### Mantle of Whispers
*6th-level College of Whispers feature*

You can adopt a slain humanoid's persona. When a humanoid dies within 30 feet of you, you can magically capture its shadow using your Reaction. You retain this shadow until you use it or you finish a long rest.

You can use the shadow as an Action. When you do so, it vanishes, magically transforming into a disguise that appears on you. You now look like the dead person, but healthy and alive. This disguise lasts for 1 hour or until you end it as a bonus action.

While you're in the disguise, you gain access to all information that the humanoid would freely share with a casual acquaintance. Such information includes general details on its background and personal life, but doesn't include secrets. The information is enough that you can pass yourself off as the person by drawing on its memories. You retain this general information after the feature ends, as a normal memory.

Creatures can see through this disguise by succeeding on a Wisdom (Insight) check contested by your Charisma (Deception) check. You gain a +5 bonus to this check. The disguise can also be removed with Dispel Magic. It is considered a spell of a level equal to the highest-level spell slot you can cast when resisting the effect.

Once you capture a shadow with this feature, you can't capture another one with it until you finish a short or long rest. 

## LEVEL 10: Shadow Lore

You gain the ability to weave dark magic into your words and tap into a creature’s deepest fears. As an action, you magically whisper a phrase that only one creature of your choice within 30 feet of you can hear. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC. It automatically succeeds if it doesn’t share a language with you or if it can’t hear you. 

On a failed save, the target is charmed by you for the next 8 hours or until you or your allies attack or damage it. It interprets the whispers as a description of its most mortifying secret. While you gain no knowledge of this secret, the target is convinced you know it. While charmed in this way, the creature obeys your commands for fear that you will reveal its secret. It won’t risk its life for you or fight for you, unless it was already inclined to do so. It grants you favors and gifts it would offer to a close friend.

When the effect ends, the creature has no understanding of why it held you in such fear. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest, unless you expend a level 3+ spell slot to restore it (no action required). 

#### Trove of Shadows
*14th-level College of Whispers feature*

When you capture a shadow using your Mantle of Whispers feature, you also learn one secret the captured shadow knew in life. This secret can be personal, professional, or simply gossip. The DM determines the secret, and it does not have to be true, only something the shadow thinks is true...but it must be something it considered a valuable secret.

In addition, when you use your Mantle of Whispers feature, you can assume the disguise of any humanoid whose shadow you have captured in the past, not just your most recently captured shadow.

The disguise created by Mantle of Whispers now lasts until you finish a short or long rest, it is dispelled, or until you end it as a bonus action.
